5. Cancellation & Rescheduling Policy
Cancellation by Client:
  • More than 60 days before event: Retainer is forfeited; no additional fee.
  • 31–60 days before event: 50% of total contract value is due.
  • 30 days or fewer before event: 100% of total contract value is due.
Rescheduling by Client:

One (1) complimentary rescheduling allowed if the new date is available and notice is given at least 30 days in advance. Subsequent reschedules are subject to a $100 rescheduling fee.

Cancellation by MC:

In the unlikely event the MC must cancel, a full refund of all payments received will be issued and the MC will make reasonable efforts to provide a qualified referral.

6. Client Responsibilities
  • Provide a finalized run-of-show no later than 10 days before each event.
  • Ensure the MC has access to the venue at least 60 minutes before program start.
  • Provide meal service for the MC during the reception.
  • Provide a working microphone system (wired or wireless) through the venue or DJ.
  • Communicate any family-specific sensitivities, name pronunciations, or special requests in advance.
6b. Event Planner Coordination

If the Client has retained an event planner or coordinator, the MC acknowledges their role in the day-of logistics. However, the MC takes binding direction exclusively from the primary Client contact named in Section 1 of this agreement. The MC is not obligated to follow instructions from the event planner that conflict with this contract or the agreed-upon run-of-show, unless the Client provides written authorization in advance.

The MC shall not be held liable for any disruptions, delays, or program changes that result from miscommunication between the Client, their event planner, and other vendors. Any last-minute changes to the program requested by the event planner on the day of the event must be approved by the Client directly.

7. Media & Content Release

By signing this agreement, the Client grants the MC full permission to capture, use, and share behind-the-scenes content, highlight moments, and event footage for social media and promotional purposes. This release is a required condition of service and is non-negotiable.

8. Limitation of Liability

The MC's total liability to the Client under this agreement, regardless of the nature of the claim (contract, tort, negligence, or otherwise), shall not exceed the total fees paid by the Client under this agreement. The MC shall not be liable for any indirect, incidental, special, consequential, or punitive damages of any kind, including but not limited to loss of enjoyment, emotional distress, lost profits, or reputational harm.

The MC is expressly not responsible for:
  • Delays, interruptions, or changes to the program caused by other vendors (DJ, photographer, caterer, florist, etc.), venue staff, or venue management.
  • Technical failures of sound systems, microphones, speakers, or audio-visual equipment not owned or operated by the MC.
  • Schedule overruns caused by late arrivals of the bridal party, guests, or family members.
  • Changes to the run-of-show made by the Client, family members, or venue staff on the day of the event without prior notice to the MC.
  • Miscommunications arising from instructions given by persons other than the primary contact named in this contract.
  • Any outcomes resulting from incomplete, inaccurate, or late delivery of the run-of-show by the Client.
  • Acts of God, extreme weather, natural disasters, power outages, public health emergencies, government-mandated restrictions, or any other force majeure event beyond the MC's reasonable control.
  • The subjective quality, satisfaction, or enjoyment of the event by any individual guest or family member.
  • Conduct, behavior, or actions of guests, family members, or third parties during the event.

The Client acknowledges that event hosting involves live, unscripted moments and that minor deviations from the planned program do not constitute a breach of this agreement. The MC commits to professional best-effort performance under the conditions present on the day of the event.

9. Governing Law & Dispute Resolution

This agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Illinois, without regard to its conflict of law provisions. Any disputes arising out of or relating to this agreement shall first be submitted to good-faith mediation between both parties before any legal action is pursued. If mediation fails, disputes shall be resolved exclusively in the courts of Cook or DuPage County, Illinois.

The Client agrees that any claim or cause of action arising from this agreement must be filed within one (1) year of the event date, after which any such claim is permanently barred.

10. Force Majeure

Neither party shall be held liable for failure to perform any obligation under this agreement if such failure results from circumstances beyond that party's reasonable control, including but not limited to: acts of God, extreme weather events, natural disasters, fire, flood, pandemic, epidemic, government-imposed restrictions or shutdowns, civil unrest, power outages, or any other event that makes performance impossible or commercially impractical.

In the event of a force majeure, the MC will make every reasonable effort to fulfill the contracted services or provide an alternative arrangement. If the event is cancelled due to force majeure, the retainer shall be retained by the MC as compensation for reserved time and preparation. Any payments beyond the retainer will be refunded on a pro-rated basis.

11. Indemnification

The Client agrees to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Absar Najam, his agents, and representatives from and against any and all claims, damages, losses, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorney's fees) arising out of or relating to: (a) the Client's breach of any provision of this agreement; (b) the acts or omissions of the Client, their guests, or family members during the event; (c) any claim by a third party related to the event that is not directly caused by the MC's own gross negligence or willful misconduct.

12. MC Punctuality & Late Arrival

The MC commits to arriving at the venue no later than sixty (60) minutes before the agreed program start time. In the event of an unforeseen delay (traffic, emergency, or other circumstance beyond the MC's control), the MC will notify the Client's primary contact as soon as possible.

A grace period of thirty (30) minutes is understood for circumstances outside the MC's control. If the MC is unable to perform due to a verified emergency and no qualified substitute can be arranged, a full refund of all payments received will be issued. The MC's liability in such cases is limited to a refund of fees paid and does not extend to any additional damages, losses, or costs incurred by the Client.

13. Payment Terms & Late Fees

The non-refundable retainer is due upon signing this agreement and secures the MC's availability for the event date(s). The remaining balance is due no later than seven (7) days prior to the first event date.

Late Payment:
  • The remaining balance is due no later than seven (7) days before the event. If payment is not received by this deadline, a one-time late fee of $50 will be applied immediately.
  • If the balance plus late fee remains unpaid 72 hours before the event, an additional $50 fee will be applied.
  • If the balance remains unpaid 24 hours before the event, the MC reserves the right to withhold services until payment is received in full.
  • Failure to pay does not entitle the Client to a refund of the retainer.

Payment methods accepted: Cash  |  Zelle  |  Venmo  |  Check (payable to Absar Najam). All payments are final and non-reversible once processed.

14. Travel & Distance Fee

Events located within thirty (30) miles of Streamwood, Illinois are included in the base contract fee. For events located more than thirty (30) miles from Streamwood, a travel fee will be assessed as follows:

  • 31–60 miles: $75 travel fee
  • 61–100 miles: $150 travel fee
  • Over 100 miles: $200 + $0.67/mile (IRS standard mileage rate) beyond 100 miles from Streamwood, IL
  • Out-of-state events: Travel fee to be mutually agreed upon in writing prior to contract signing and added as an addendum to this agreement.
  • Overnight stay required (100+ miles one-way or out-of-state): Client is responsible for arranging and covering the cost of hotel accommodations for the MC and his immediate family (spouse and one child), at a reasonable hotel near the venue, for the duration required.

The agreed travel fee for this booking, if applicable, is reflected in the total fee amount in Section 3. Any out-of-state travel arrangements will be confirmed via written addendum signed by both parties before the booking is considered finalized.

15. Outdoor Events & Weather

For events held fully or partially outdoors, the Client is responsible for ensuring adequate shelter, shade, or weather protection for the MC and all audio-visual equipment. The MC reserves the right to pause or relocate the program if outdoor conditions pose a safety risk, including but not limited to lightning, extreme heat (above 100°F), heavy rain, or high winds.

If inclement weather forces an event to be cancelled or relocated after the MC has arrived on-site, the full contract fee is still owed. The MC shall not be held liable for program disruptions caused by weather conditions.

16. Confidentiality & Non-Disparagement

Both parties agree to keep the specific financial terms of this agreement confidential and not to disclose them publicly or to third parties without mutual written consent.

The Client agrees not to make any false, misleading, or disparaging statements about the MC, his services, or his business — whether publicly, on social media, or in review platforms — that are not based on factual, documented occurrences. The MC agrees to the same standard regarding the Client. This clause survives the termination of this agreement.

17. Review & Referral Request

The MC kindly requests that satisfied Clients consider sharing an honest review or testimonial via Instagram DM or text message to be featured on @MC_Absar on Instagram. Reviews and testimonials help support a small business and are deeply appreciated. There is no obligation to do so, and this request has no bearing on the services provided.

If the Client refers another couple or client who books the MC's services, the referring Client will receive a $50 credit toward any future booking as a thank-you gesture.

18. Program Overtime

The MC's services are contracted for the duration specified in the event details of this agreement. If the event program extends beyond the agreed end time due to scheduling delays, extended speeches, late starts, or any other reason, an overtime rate of $150 per hour (or any portion thereof) will apply and is due within 48 hours of the event.

The MC will notify the primary client contact when approaching the contracted end time. Continued services beyond that point constitute acceptance of the overtime rate. Overtime fees are not subject to the retainer or existing balance — they are billed separately following the event.

19. Alcohol & Intoxicated Guests

The MC is not responsible for managing the behavior of intoxicated guests. If the conduct of intoxicated guests disrupts the program, creates an unsafe environment, or interferes with the MC's ability to perform, the MC reserves the right to pause, modify, or restructure the program at his discretion without liability or reduction in fees.

The Client is solely responsible for ensuring appropriate crowd management and security at the venue. Any disruptions caused by guest behavior that materially impact the program do not constitute a breach of this agreement by the MC.

20. MC’s Right to Refuse or End Services

The MC reserves the right to immediately cease services without refund if he is subjected to verbal abuse, threats, harassment, or physically unsafe conditions by the Client, their guests, or any third party at the event. The MC's personal safety and dignity are non-negotiable conditions of service.

In such an event, the MC will make reasonable efforts to notify the primary client contact before departing. No portion of fees paid shall be refunded, and the MC shall not be liable for any damages resulting from early termination under these circumstances.

21. Guest Count & Event Scale

The Client agrees to disclose a reasonable estimate of expected guest attendance at the time of booking. If the actual guest count or event scale significantly exceeds the disclosed estimate (by more than 50%), the MC reserves the right to renegotiate the contract fee to reflect the increased scope of services required.

Any pricing adjustment due to event scale must be agreed upon in writing at least 14 days before the event. Failure to disclose accurate guest count information may result in additional fees.

22. Run-of-Show Submission & Consequence

The Client is required to submit a finalized run-of-show no later than ten (10) days before each event date. If the Client fails to submit the run-of-show by this deadline, the MC will proceed with a best-effort program based on available information and standard event flow.

The MC shall not be held liable for omissions, missed announcements, incorrect name pronunciations, or program errors resulting from a late or incomplete run-of-show submission. Late submission does not entitle the Client to a reduction in fees or damages of any kind.

23. Dress Code & Professional Appearance

The MC will dress in professional formal attire appropriate to the event (suit, sherwani, or equivalent) unless a specific dress code or theme is communicated by the Client at least 14 days in advance. The MC reserves the right to decline dress code requests that are impractical, culturally inappropriate, or incompatible with his professional image.

24. Entire Agreement

This document, along with any signed addenda, constitutes the entire agreement between both parties. Any modifications must be agreed upon in writing and signed by both parties.
